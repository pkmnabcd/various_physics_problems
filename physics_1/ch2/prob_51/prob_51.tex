\begin{flushleft}

Say that your speed is $v_1=9.0\,\unitSpeed$. Since your speed is 20\% faster than your brother's speed $v_2$, this implies that $v_1=\frac{6}{5}v_2$ or $v_2=\frac{5}{6}v_1$.
This implies that $v_2=7.5\,\unitSpeed$.\\~\\

To determine how much time it takes to displace between two locations at a constant speed, use the equation
$$v=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$$
or
$$\Delta t=\frac{\Delta x}{v}$$

For us, $\Delta x=100\,\text{m}$.
Therefore, $\Delta t_1=\frac{100}{9.0}\,\text{s}=11.1\,\text{s}$ and $\Delta t_2=\frac{100}{7.5}\,\text{s}=13.3\,\text{s}$.
For both people to arrive at the same time, you need to know the difference between the amount of time it takes the two people to arrive.
$\Delta t=t_2-t_1=2.2\,\text{s}$, you have to start running $2.2\,\text{s}$ after.




\end{flushleft}
