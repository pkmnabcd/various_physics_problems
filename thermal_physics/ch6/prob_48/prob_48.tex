For this problem, we must derive the entropy (and later the chemical potential) of a diatomic gas near room temperature.
For an ideal gas under these conditions, we have the following equation for entropy.
\begin{equation}
    S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]-\partDeriv{F_\text{int}}{T}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
    F_\text{int}=-NkT\ln(Z_\text{int})
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
    v_Q=\left(\frac{h}{\sqrt{2\pi mkT}}\right)^3
\end{equation}
For our purposes, we're given that $Z_\text{int}=Z_eZ_\text{rot}$, where $Z_e$ is the degeneracy of the ground state and $Z_\text{rot}=\frac{kT}{2\epsilon}$ for diatomic molecules with identical atoms at normal temperatures.\\~\\

We can find $S$ by plugging in $F_\text{int}$ into the derivative.
$$S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]-\partDeriv{F_\text{int}}{T}$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]-\partDeriv{}{T}(-NkT\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot}))$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]+Nk\left[\partDeriv{}{T}(T\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot}))\right]$$
$Z_\text{rot}$ depends on $T$, so this means the following results.
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]+Nk\left[\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot})+\frac{T}{Z_eZ_\text{rot}}\cdot Z_e\partDeriv{Z_\text{rot}}{T}\right]$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]+Nk\left[\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot})+\frac{T}{Z_eZ_\text{rot}}\cdot \frac{Z_eZ_\text{rot}}{T}\right]$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]+Nk\left[\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot})+1\right]$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\ln(Z_eZ_\text{rot})+\frac{7}{2}\right]$$
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{VZ_eZ_\text{rot}}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{7}{2}\right]$$\\~\\

Now that we have an equation for $S$, we can calculate the entropy of a mole of oxygen ($N=6.022\times10^{23}$, $\epsilon=0.00018\,\text{eV}$, and $Z_e=3$) at room temperature and standard pressure.
First, we were not given a volume, so we must use $PV=NkT$ to replace $V$ in the equation.
$$\implies S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{kTZ_eZ_\text{rot}}{Pv_Q}\right)+\frac{7}{2}\right]$$
Recall that $T=300\,\text{K}$ and $P=1.0133\times10^{5}\,\text{N/m}^2$.\\~\\
