\begin{flushleft}


For this problem, we must derive the entropy (and later the chemical potential) of a diatomic gas near room temperature.
For an ideal gas under these conditions, we have the following equation for entropy.
\begin{equation}
    S=Nk\left[\ln\left(\frac{V}{Nv_Q}\right)+\frac{5}{2}\right]-\partDeriv{F_\text{int}}{T}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
    F_\text{int}=-NkT\ln(Z_\text{int})
\end{equation}
For our purposes, we're given that $Z_\text{int}=Z_eZ_\text{rot}$, where $Z_e$ is the degeneracy of the ground state and $Z_\text{rot}=\frac{kT}{2\epsilon}$ for diatomic molecules with identical atoms at normal temperatures.



\end{flushleft}
