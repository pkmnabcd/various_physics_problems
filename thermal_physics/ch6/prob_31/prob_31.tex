\author{Gabriel Decker}

\begin{flushleft}

In this problem, we begin with a degree of freedom - energy relation: $E(q)=c|q|$. Our goal is to calculate the average energy $\overline{E}$.
We'll need the following equations to do so.
\begin{equation}
    Z=\sum_se^{-\beta E(s)}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \overline{E}=-\frac{1}{Z}\partDeriv{Z}{\beta}
\end{equation}\\~\\

First, let's plug in our energy relation into the equation for $Z$.
$$Z=\sum_se^{-\beta E(s)}$$
$$\implies Z=\sum_qe^{-\beta c|q|}$$
$$\implies Z=\sum_{q=-\infty}^\infty e^{-\beta c|q|}$$
We can multiply and divide by $\Delta q$ (assuming a fixed width between $q$s) and then assume $\Delta q\rightarrow dq$ to transform the infinite series into an integral.
$$\implies Z=\frac{1}{\Delta q}\sum_{q=-\infty}^\infty e^{-\beta c|q|}\Delta q$$
$$\implies Z=\frac{1}{\Delta q}\int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-\beta c|q|}\,dq$$
Since $e^{-\beta c|q|}$ is an even function, we can replace this integral with the following.
$$\implies Z=\frac{2}{\Delta q}\int_0^\infty e^{-\beta c|q|}\,dq$$
At this range of values, $|q|=q$ always, so we can replace that in the integral.
$$\implies Z=\frac{2}{\Delta q}\int_0^\infty e^{-\beta cq}\,dq$$
Let's make a change of variables: $x=\beta cq$, so $\implies dx=\beta c\,dq$.
$$\implies Z=\frac{2}{\beta c\Delta q}\int_0^\infty e^{-x}\,dx$$
$$\implies Z=-\frac{2}{\beta c\Delta q}e^{-x}\Big|_0^\infty$$
$$\implies Z=\frac{2}{\beta c\Delta q}$$
$$\implies Z=C\beta^{-1}$$\\~\\

Now that we have an expression for $Z$, we can find $\overline{E}$.
$$\overline{E}=-\frac{1}{Z}\partDeriv{Z}{\beta}$$
$$\implies\overline{E}=-\frac{\beta}{C}\partDeriv{}{\beta}(C\beta^{-1})$$
$$\implies\overline{E}=\frac{C\beta}{C\beta^2}$$
$$\implies\overline{E}=\frac{1}{\beta}$$
Since $\beta=1/kT$,
$$\implies\overline{E}=kT$$



\end{flushleft}
